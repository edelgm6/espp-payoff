Stock data

We care about two datapoints for a given stock:

  • Latest price
  • Annualized volatility
Latest price

The latest price matters for two reasons:

  • Shares cap: You can only purchase 1000 shares in total, irrespective of the stock price. If the ~$14.70, you can deploy less capital and therefore get a lower return
  • Proximity to $0: The closer a stock price is to $0, the more likely it is that it can end up at $0!

Annualized volatility

Annualized volatility is the variance (i.e., the square of the standard deviation) of the daily percent change in the stock price. It's critically important for the value of the ESPP.

To understand why, you first need to understand that an ESPP behaves similarly to a call option — i.e., you have capped downside but potentially unlimited upside. Given the capped downside, an option is more valuable when the expected spread in values from its current value is highest.
